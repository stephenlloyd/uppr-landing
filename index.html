Sound familiar?

"The plan doesn't match my gym"

Set up your equipment once. Every exercise, every suggestion — filtered to what you actually have. Travelling? Switch profiles mid-workout. Exercises swap automatically.

"I never know what weight to use"

Discovery mode finds your working weight for any new exercise in 2 sets. After that, the app tracks your 1RM and suggests weight + reps based on your actual strength, not a formula from a spreadsheet.

"Am I actually getting stronger?"

Every set updates your estimated 1RM. Strength trends, volume tracking, and progress tiers show you exactly where you stand — not just how many times you showed up.

"I want flexibility, not a rigid schedule"

Skip a day, add a bonus workout, swap an exercise just for today. No calendar. No guilt. Your plan is a queue that moves when you do.

"How do I know my plan is actually good?"

Every plan is built against research-backed volume targets. Every muscle group is covered — focus muscles get growth-level volume, everything else gets enough to maintain. You choose the goal, the app makes sure nothing gets missed.

"I don't have time for an hour workout"

You don't need one. What matters is hitting your weekly volume — not when you do it. Split it across short sessions, do it all at once, train at 6am or 11pm. The app doesn't care when. It just tracks that you did.

Questions

What makes this different from Hevy or Strong?

Hevy and Strong are excellent loggers — great for tracking what you did. uppr also tells you what to do next. It estimates your 1RM from every set, suggests weight and reps based on your actual strength, and adapts your plan based on feedback. Think of it as the difference between a notebook and a coach.

How does it compare to RP Hypertrophy or Juggernaut?

RP and Juggernaut pioneered science-based training apps and they're genuinely good. We added equipment awareness (they don't know what gym you're in), AI plan import (paste any coach plan), discovery mode (2 sets to find your working weight), multiple gym profiles, and a free tier that's actually usable. The comparison table is honest — we all have video libraries now, but we beat them on equipment awareness and flexibility.

Do I need to understand RIR or periodisation?

No. RIR is the engine under the hood. You just log your sets, answer "how was that?" and the app handles progression. If you do understand it — great, the RIR target is shown on every exercise so you know exactly what you're aiming for.

What's discovery mode?

When you do an exercise for the first time, the app runs you through 2 quick calibration sets — one light, one heavy. From that it estimates your 1RM and calculates your working weight. Takes about 4 minutes. After that, every suggestion is based on your actual strength.

Does it work offline?

Yes. The workout screen works with zero internet. Your data syncs when you're back online. No dropped sets because you trained in a basement gym with no signal.

Can I import my coach's plan?

Yes — any format. Use the built-in prompt builder with any AI. It converts spreadsheets, PDFs, text messages — whatever your coach sent you. Their programming is preserved, the app adds equipment filtering and weekly progression.

What if I just want a quick workout?

Tap Quick Workout, pick your time and target muscles. The Smart Coach analyses what you've been neglecting and builds a session that fills the gaps. It uses exercises you've done before (no discovery overhead) and respects your equipment and preferences.

Is my data private?

Yes. No ad trackers. No data sales. No third-party analytics. Export everything or delete your account at any time.
